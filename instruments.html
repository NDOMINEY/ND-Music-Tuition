Drums are the heart and sole of most rhythm sections in a musical ensemble. Please have a listen below to some tracks that highlight some inspiration tracks to get your education started!

The traditional kit layout that you will find in most bands consists of a bass drum, snare and toms. There will also commonly be a hi-hat cymbal and possibly a crash and ride cymbal. There are multiple routes to go down for your first purchase whether brand new or second hand, one of the first decision for you to make is whether to get an acoustic or electic kit.

Bass guitars are firmly apart of the rhythm section and work in partnership with the drums to maintain the tempo and beat. Bass is also able to add those low notes to hold the groove together, and often at the element to that often you find bobbing your head too.

Bass guitar come in varying sizes and colours so you may find the choice overwhelming. The best thing to do is go to a music store and try some out! With an electric bass guitar you will also need to invest in an amp, which sometimes you can get in a package deal.

Guitars can be both in the rhythm and lead sections and often interchange between the two roles. Musical feel is important and a great sounding guitar piece can range from the most simplest to complicated music.

There is a vast variety options when it comes to picking a guitar - acoustic or electric. You can get all inclusive starter packs which will include everything you need, including an amp if you choose an electric guitar.
